Litigation lawyer here. Consider the following hypothetical scenario: An individual imports a man-eating lion from Africa and confines it in their backyard using only a three-foot chain link fence. Predictably, the lion escapes, breaches the inadequate barrier, and proceeds to attack neighboring residents. Ultimately, a team of hunters intervenes, kills the lion, and thereby ends the immediate threat.

In my jurisdiction, this fact pattern would likely constitute second-degree manslaughter, which includes the unlawful killing of a human being resulting from the act, procurement, or culpable negligence of another.

A similar legal analysis would likely apply in a case involving the summoning of a dangerous entity, such as a demon, where the summoner failed to employ appropriate safeguards to contain or restrain it.
